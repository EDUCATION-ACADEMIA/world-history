Why did mass media culture become a feature of life during the later industrial era?

(A) The invention of the car allowed people to go to places that they never had been.

(B) People worked more and had more time to talk to coworkers.

(C) People were less educated and were swayed by advertisements.

(D) ** Communication improvements made it possible to reach a larger audience of people.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -With the transition to the industrial age in the 18th century, large populations headed to urban areas, creating mass audiences of all economic classes seeking information and entertainment. Printing technology was at the heart of modernization WHICH led to magazines, newspapers, the telegraph, and the telephone.

Concept note-2: -Cultural diffusion used to happen through people-to-people contact. Mass media technology has changed the very nature of cultural diffusion, by widely propagating cultures and norms across wider audiences. That is the reason why modern society picks up all new trends, fashion, and fads from mass media.

Concept note-3: -The media plays a very important role in ensuring that societal norms, ideologies and customs are disseminated. Socialization has been made possible and much simpler because of the media. Through socialization, different societies are able to share languages, traditions, customs, roles and values.
