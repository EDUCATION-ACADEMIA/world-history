Where did the Renaissance begin?

(A) China

(B) Paris

(C) London

(D) ** Florence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Renaissance started in Florence, Italy, a place with a rich cultural history where wealthy citizens could afford to support budding artists. Members of the powerful Medici family, which ruled Florence for more than 60 years, were famous backers of the movement.

Concept note-2: -The Renaissance began in Italy in the mid 1300s and spread to other parts of Europe in the 1400s and 1500s. Let’s look more closely at this “great rebirth” of interest in classical art and learning. Then we’ll use art to explore the link between the Renaissance and the classical world.

Concept note-3: -There is a consensus that the Renaissance began in Florence, Italy, in the 14th century.

Concept note-4: -The Renaissance Began in Italy, Because Italy Was the Birthplace of Antiquity. The Renaissance began in Italy, the birthplace of the Roman Empire. Following the fall of the empire in the 4th century, and the subsequent dark ages, the incredible art and ideas of Roman times were temporarily lost.

Concept note-5: -Florence, where the Italian Renaissance began, was an independent republic. It was also a banking and commercial capital and, after London and Constantinople, the third-largest city in Europe. Wealthy Florentines flaunted their money and power by becoming patrons, or supporters, of artists and intellectuals.
