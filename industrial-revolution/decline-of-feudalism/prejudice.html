Prejudice

(A) ** An unjustifiable attitude toward a group and its members, generally involves stereotyped beliefs, negative feelings and discriminatory actions

(B) To blame for, make a claim of wrong doing

(C) Political changes to make things better

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Prejudice is unjustifiable and negative attitudes toward an individual or group based on reinforced misinformation about a social group. Prejudice is cultivated by stereotypes.

Concept note-2: -Prejudice An unjustifiable attitude toward a group and its members Generally involves stereotyped beliefs, negative feelings, and a predisposition to discriminatory action Stereotype A generalized belief about a group of people All Asian kids are great violinists…

Concept note-3: -Prejudice means “prejudgment.” It is an unjustifiable and usually negative attitude toward a group and its members-who often are people of a particular racial or ethnic group, gender, sexual orientation, or belief system. What are the factors involved in prejudice? hostility or fear.

Concept note-4: -Prejudice is associated with attitudes, while discrimination is associated with behavior. Negative actions directed toward members of a social group. Discrimination is best described as actions resulting from prejudicial beliefs, from avoidance to exclusion, to aggression.
