What was the name of the 1973 war in which Israel held off Arab attacks?

(A) Hannukah War

(B) ** Yom Kippur War

(C) Israeli-Arab War

(D) Ramadan War

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Iraqi forces soon joined the war, and Syria received support from Jordan. After several days, Israel was fully mobilized, and the Israel Defense Forces began beating back the Arab gains at a heavy cost to soldiers and equipment.

Concept note-2: -The war was so called because it started on the holiest day in the Jewish calendar, the Day of Atonement (October 6, 1973). It came almost as a complete surprise and warning notice was given too late for an orderly call-up of the reserves before zero hour.

Concept note-3: -The Yom Kippur War, also known as the Ramadan War, the October War, the 1973 Arab窶的sraeli War, or the Fourth Arab窶的sraeli War, was an armed conflict fought from October 6 to 25, 1973 between Israel and a coalition of Arab states led by Egypt and Syria.

Concept note-4: -Yom Kippur War, also called the October War, the Ramadan War, the Arab-Israeli war of October 1973, or the Fourth Arab-Israeli War, fourth of the Arab-Israeli wars, which was initiated by Egypt and Syria on October 6, 1973, on the Jewish holy day of Yom Kippur.

Concept note-5: -On October 6, 1973, Egypt and Syria launched a coordinated surprise attack on Israeli forces in the Sinai and the Golan Heights. Israel ultimately repelled the attack and regained lost ground, but only after the United States made the decision to supply the Israeli military.
