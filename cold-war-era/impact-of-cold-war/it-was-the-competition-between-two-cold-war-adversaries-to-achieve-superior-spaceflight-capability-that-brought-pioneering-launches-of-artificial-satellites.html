IMPACT OF COLD WAR

It was the competition between two Cold War adversaries to achieve superior spaceflight capability that brought pioneering launches of artificial satellites.

(A) The Cuban Missile Crisis

(B) ** The Space Race

(C) Vietnam War

(D) Korean War

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Space Race was a 20th-century competition between two Cold War rivals, the United States and the Soviet Union, to achieve superior spaceflight capability. It had its origins in the ballistic missile-based nuclear arms race between the two nations following World War II.

Concept note-2: -The “space race” was a Cold War competition between the United States and the Soviet Union to develop aerospace capabilities, including artificial satellites, unmanned space probes, and human spaceflight.

Concept note-3: -The Space Race played a significant part in the Cold War as the Americans and Soviets competed to prove their technological and intellectual superiority by becoming the first nation to put a human into space. From beginning to end, the world’s attention was captivated by this contest for dominance.

Concept note-4: -The Soviet program was also responsible for leading the first interplanetary probes to Venus and Mars and made successful soft landings on these planets in the 1960s and 1970s. It put the first space station into low Earth orbit in 1971 and the first modular space station in 1986.
