What was the meeting between the Allied leaders Churchill, Roosevelt, and Stalin in 1945, where theleaders planned the final stages of World War II and agreed on how to divide up parts of Europe after the war?

(A) Oslo Agreement

(B) Berlin Conference

(C) ** Yalta Conference

(D) Geneva Conference

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Yalta Conference was a meeting of three World War II allies: U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt, British Prime Minister Winston Churchill and Soviet Premier Joseph Stalin. The trio met in February 1945 in the resort city of Yalta, located along the Black Sea coast of the Crimean Peninsula.

Concept note-2: -The Yalta Conference, 1945 At Yalta, U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt, British Prime Minister Winston Churchill, and Soviet Premier Joseph Stalin made important decisions regarding the future progress of the war and the postwar world.

Concept note-3: -Agreement to the priority of the unconditional surrender of Nazi Germany. After the war, Germany and Berlin would be split into four occupied zones. Stalin agreed that France would have a fourth occupation zone in Germany if it was formed from the American and the British zones.

Concept note-4: -The Potsdam Conference, 1945. The Big Three-Soviet leader Joseph Stalin, British Prime Minister Winston Churchill (replaced on July 26 by Prime Minister Clement Attlee), and U.S. President Harry Truman-met in Potsdam, Germany, from July 17 to August 2, 1945, to negotiate terms for the end of World War II.

Concept note-5: -The Yalta Conference. In February 1945, when they were confident of an Allied victory, U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt, British Prime Minister Winston Churchill and Stalin met near Yalta, Crimea, to discuss the reorganization of post-WWII Europe.
