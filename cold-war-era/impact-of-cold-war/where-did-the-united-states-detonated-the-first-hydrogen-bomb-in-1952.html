Where did the United States detonated the first hydrogen bomb in 1952?

(A) Palmyra Atoll

(B) Ataul Atoll

(C) ** Eniwetok Atoll

(D) Bikini Atoll

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On November 1, 1952, the United States successfully detonated “Mike, ” the world’s first hydrogen bomb, on the Eniwetok Atoll in the Pacific Marshall Islands.

Concept note-2: -1 November 1952 | US tests the first hydrogen bomb The United States detonates the first hydrogen bomb at Enewetak Atoll in the Marshall Islands.

Concept note-3: -On Eniwetok Atoll in the Marshall Islands, the United States, on May 12th, 1951, detonated the first hydrogen bomb. The bomb was based on the combination of a nuclei of heavy hydrogen, called deuterium, and the process of fission.

Concept note-4: -The first fusion bomb was tested by the United States in Operation Ivy on November 1, 1952, on Elugelab Island in the Enewatak Atoll of the Marshall Islands.

Concept note-5: -It was successfully detonated during Operation Ivy, on November 1, 1952, at Enewetak. The explosion achieved a yield of 10.4 megatons (million tons), 500 times larger than the Nagasaki bomb, and it produced a crater 1, 900 metres (6, 240 feet) in diameter and 50 metres (164 feet) deep.
