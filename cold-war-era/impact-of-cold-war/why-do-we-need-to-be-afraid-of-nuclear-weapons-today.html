Why do we need to be afraid of nuclear weapons today?

(A) ** Because countries, including North Korea, have nuclear weapons.

(B) We don’t need to be afraid, because they have all been destroyed.

(C) We don’t need to be afraid, because only the United States has nuclear weapons.

(D) We don’t need to be afraid, because nuclear weapons aren’t very powerful.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -North Korea’s long-range missile and nuclear programs represent the region’s most immediate security challenge. Any major instability or conflict on the Korean Peninsula would have severe strategic, economic and humanitarian repercussions.

Concept note-2: -Pyongyang has recently escalated nuclear threats and threatened an “unprecedentedly” strong response to annual U.S.-South Korea military drills, which it views as preparation for an invasion.

Concept note-3: -Nuclear weapons are the most dangerous weapons on earth. One can destroy a whole city, potentially killing millions, and jeopardizing the natural environment and lives of future generations through its long-term catastrophic effects. The dangers from such weapons arise from their very existence.
