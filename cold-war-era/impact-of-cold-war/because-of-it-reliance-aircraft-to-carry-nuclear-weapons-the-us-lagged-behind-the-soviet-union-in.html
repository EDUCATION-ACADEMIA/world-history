IMPACT OF COLD WAR

(A) the hydrogen bomb testing

(B) spy plane technology

(C) ** missile development

(D) destroyer and cruiser construction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -After many long and difficult meetings, Kennedy decided to place a naval blockade, or a ring of ships, around Cuba. The aim of this “quarantine, ” as he called it, was to prevent the Soviets from bringing in more military supplies. He demanded the removal of the missiles already there and the destruction of the sites.

Concept note-2: -US officials were worried that one of the Cuban or Soviet SAMs in Cuba might shoot down a CIA U-2, initiating another international incident.

Concept note-3: -The United States first responded to the Soviet Union’s possession of atomic weapons by creating a hydrogen bomb, which was much more powerful than a fission bomb. When the Soviet Union exploded their first hydrogen bomb in 1953, the US responded by increasing their nuclear arsenal.

Concept note-4: -On August 5, 1963, after more than eight years of difficult negotiations, the United States, the United Kingdom, and the Soviet Union signed the Limited Nuclear Test Ban Treaty.
