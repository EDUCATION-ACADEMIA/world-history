How did the conflict in Vietnam end?

(A) ** Cease-fire agreement in which U.S. troops withdrew

(B) Both sides won

(C) United States occupied Japan

(D) Soviets removed the missiles in Cuba in response to a U.S. blockade

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Finally, in January 1973, representatives of the United States, North and South Vietnam, and the Vietcong signed a peace agreement in Paris, ending the direct U.S. military involvement in the Vietnam War.

Concept note-2: -The remaining Americans escaped in a series of frantic air-and sealifts with Vietnamese friends and coworkers. A military government was instituted, and on July 2, 1976, the country was officially united as the Socialist Republic of Vietnam with its capital in Hanoi. Saigon was renamed Ho Chi Minh City.

Concept note-3: -The January accords, titled the “Agreement on Ending the War and Restoring Peace in Vietnam, ” neither ended the war (except for the United States) nor restored the peace. A little over 2 years later, 30 North Vietnamese divisions conquered the South and restored peace in Vietnam.

Concept note-4: -Communist forces ended the war by seizing control of South Vietnam in 1975, and the country was unified as the Socialist Republic of Vietnam the following year.

Concept note-5: -The Paris Peace Accords, (Vietnamese: Hiệp định Paris về Việt Nam), officially the Agreement on Ending the War and Restoring Peace in Viet Nam (Hiệp định về chấm dứt chiến tranh, lập lại hòa bình ở Việt Nam), was a peace treaty signed on January 27, 1973, to establish peace in Vietnam and end the Vietnam War.
