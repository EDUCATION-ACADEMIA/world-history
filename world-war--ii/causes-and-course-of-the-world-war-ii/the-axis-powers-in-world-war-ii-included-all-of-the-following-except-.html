The Axis Powers in World War II included all of the following EXCEPT ____

(A) Germany

(B) Italy

(C) ** Poland

(D) Japan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Axis powers (Germany, Italy, Japan) were opposed by the Allied Powers (led by Great Britain, the United States, and the Soviet Union). Five other nations joined the Axis during World War II: Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, Slovakia, and Croatia.

Concept note-2: -Poland never accepted any offer of cooperation with the Axis, but it did claim a part of Czechoslovakia when the country was dissolved by two Central European Axis powers, Nazi Germany and Hungary in 1938.

Concept note-3: -France was a part of the Allied countries and not a part of the Axis powers during the second world war.

Concept note-4: -In World War Two, the Polish armed forces were the fourth largest Allied forces in Europe, after those of the Soviet Union, United States, and Britain. Poles made substantial contributions to the Allied effort throughout the war, fighting on land, sea, and in the air.
