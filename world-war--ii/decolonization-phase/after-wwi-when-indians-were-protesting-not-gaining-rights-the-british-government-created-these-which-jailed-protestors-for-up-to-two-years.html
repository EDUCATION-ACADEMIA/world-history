After WWI, when Indians were protesting not gaining rights, the British government created these which jailed protestors for up to two years.

(A) ** Rowlatt Acts

(B) Salt Acts

(C) Amritsar Massacre

(D) Government of India Act

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Act was passed by the Rowlatt Committee, presided by Sir Sydney Rowlatt. It authorized the arrest, for 2 years without trial, of any person suspected of terrorism living in British India.

Concept note-2: -Rowlatt Acts, (February 1919), legislation passed by the Imperial Legislative Council, the legislature of British India. The acts allowed certain political cases to be tried without juries and permitted internment of suspects without trial.
