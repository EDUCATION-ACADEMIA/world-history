Civil Disobedience refers to . . .

(A) Rioting

(B) Breaking the laws to draw out police to start fighting

(C) Breaking unjust laws and resisting arrest

(D) ** Breaking unjust laws and accepting punishment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is a nonviolent action engaged in by an individual who refuses to obey a law for moral or philosophical reasons. The participants in civil disobedience willfully and openly refuse to comply with a law in order to dramatize the issue that they, or the group, find unjust.

Concept note-2: -Civil disobedience can be defined as refusing to obey a law, a regulation or a power judged unjust in a peaceful manner. Civil disobedience is, therefore, a form of resistance without violence.

Concept note-3: -Civil disobedience is the active, professed refusal of a citizen to obey certain laws, demands, orders or commands of a government (or any other authority).

Concept note-4: -The breaking of the Salt Law by Mahatma Gandhi on 6 April 1930 signalled the beginning of the Civil Disobedience Movement.

Concept note-5: -The objective of the Civil Disobedience Movement was to refuse to obey certain laws, demands, orders, or commands given by the government.
