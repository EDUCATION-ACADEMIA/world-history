Which city was the symbolic point for the beginning and end of the Cold War?

(A) Sarajevo

(B) Prague

(C) Budapest

(D) ** Berlin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Berlin crisis of 1961, Cold War conflict between the Soviet Union and the United States concerning the status of the divided German city of Berlin. It culminated in the construction of the Berlin Wall in August 1961.

Concept note-2: -Running across cemeteries and along canals, zigzagging through the city streets, the Berlin Wall was a chilling symbol of the Iron Curtain that divided all of Europe between communism and democracy. Berlin was at the heart of the Cold War.

Concept note-3: -Berlin played a central role in the conflict of the Cold War. In 1949, Berlin became the capital of a regime that would prove to be the “best student” in the Soviet Bloc. The East German leaders struggled to return the city to its pre-war lustre. Reconstruction was very slow.

Concept note-4: -By October 1990, Germany was reunified, triggering the swift collapse of the other East European regimes. People celebrating the fall of the Berlin Wall. Thirteen months later, on December 25, 1991, Gorbachev resigned and the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics dissolved.

Concept note-5: -Berlin is the capital and chief urban center of Germany. Berlin was the capital of Prussia and then, from 1871, of a unified Germany. Though partitioned into East and West Berlin after World War II, the reunification of East and West Germany led to Berlin’s reinstatement as the all-German capital in 1990.
