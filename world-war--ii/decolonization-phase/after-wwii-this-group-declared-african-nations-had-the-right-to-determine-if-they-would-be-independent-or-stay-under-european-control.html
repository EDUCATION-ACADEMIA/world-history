WORLD WAR II

DECOLONIZATION PHASE

[SOURCES]
After WWII, this group declared African nations had the right to determine if they would be independent or stay under European control.

(A) ** The United Nations

(B) The League of Nations

(C) Warsaw Pact

(D) NATO

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The war helped build strong African nationalism, which resulted in a common goal for all Africans to fight for their freedom. World War II led to decolonization of Africa by affecting both Europe and Africa militarily, psychologically, politically, and economically.

Concept note-2: -After World War II, decolonization occurred for several reasons. Soldiers from the colonies who fought for the European colonial powers returned home to fight for their countriesâ€™ independence. The European colonial powers were in debt from the war and could not afford colonies.

Concept note-3: -Nevertheless, in the three years after the end of the Second World War, India, Pakistan, Burma and Ceylon all achieved independence from the British Empire, with Malaya and Singapore (as an independent republic) to follow later in 1957 and 1965 respectively.

Concept note-4: -Consumed with post-war debt, European powers were no longer able to afford the resources needed to maintain control of their African colonies. This allowed African nationalists to negotiate decolonisation very quickly and with minimal casualties.
