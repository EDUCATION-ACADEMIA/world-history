MAJOR EVENTS IN PRE REVOLUTION RUSSIA

[SOURCES]
Followers of Lenin took the name Bolsheviks after 1903. What did they call their opponents in government?

(A) ** the Mensheviks (those of the minority)

(B) the Czarists (those of the Czar)

(C) the Boyars (those of the nobility)

(D) the Monarchists (those of the crown)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They assumed the name Bolsheviks and dubbed their opponents the Mensheviks (“Those of the Minority”). Although both factions participated together in the Russian Revolution of 1905 and went through periods of apparent reconciliation (about 1906 and 1910), their differences increased.

Concept note-2: -The Menshevik were the minority group who taught that the party should be open to all. The Bolshevik were the majority group led by Vladimir Lenin. Bolsheviks were radical revolutionaries while Mensheviks were more moderate.

Concept note-3: -Menshevik, (Russian: “One of the Minority”) plural Mensheviks or Mensheviki, member of the non-Leninist wing of the Russian Social-Democratic Workers’ Party, which evolved into a separate organization.

Concept note-4: -L. Martov, pseudonym of Yuly Osipovich Tsederbaum, (born Nov. 24, 1873, Constantinople-died April 4, 1923, Berlin), leader of the Mensheviks, the non-Leninist wing of the Russian Social Democratic Workers’ Party.

Concept note-5: -In 1917, Lenin returned home to Russia and he turned everything around, he felt it was time for the soviets to take over power. Lenin also argued that the Bolshevik Party should rename themselves as the Communist Party.
