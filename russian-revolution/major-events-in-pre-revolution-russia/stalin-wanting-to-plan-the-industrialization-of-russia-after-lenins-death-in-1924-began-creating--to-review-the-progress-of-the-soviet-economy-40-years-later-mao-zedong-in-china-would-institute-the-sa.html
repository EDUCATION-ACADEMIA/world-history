[SOURCES]
Stalin, wanting to plan the industrialization of Russia after Lenin’s death in 1924, began creating ____ to review the progress of the Soviet economy. 40 years later, Mao Zedong in China would institute the same plans.

(A) Right to Work Laws

(B) Sputnik

(C) ** Five Year Plans

(D) The Peace Corps

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1928 Stalin introduced an economic policy based on a cycle of Five-Year Plans. The First Five-Year Plan called for the collectivization of agriculture and the expansion of heavy industry, like fuel extraction, energy generation, and steel production.

Concept note-2: -In the Soviet Union the first Five-Year Plan (1928–32), implemented by Joseph Stalin, concentrated on developing heavy industry and collectivizing agriculture, at the cost of a drastic fall in consumer goods.

Concept note-3: -Stalin’s First Five-Year Plan, adopted by the party in 1928, called for rapid industrialization of the economy, with an emphasis on heavy industry. It set goals that were unrealistic-a 250 percent increase in overall industrial development and a 330 percent expansion in heavy industry alone.

Concept note-4: -In many cases, the immediate effect of collectivization was the reduction of output and the cutting of the number of livestock in half. The subsequent recovery of the agricultural production was also impeded by the losses suffered by the Soviet Union during World War II and the severe drought of 1946.
