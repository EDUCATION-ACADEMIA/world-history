RUSSIAN REVOLUTION

MAJOR EVENTS IN PRE REVOLUTION RUSSIA

[SOURCES]
The major goal of Stalin’s five year plans was to

(A) Allow capitalist ventures

(B) Create an economy based on agriculture

(C) ** Industrialize the country

(D) Reduce the money spent on transporation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The First Five-Year Plan, implemented from 1928-1932, was prompted by a desire to industrialize Russia (renamed the Soviet Union) to be able to win a likely war and to reach greater consistency with the Marxist theory.

Concept note-2: -The first five year plan was created in order to initiate rapid and large-scale industrialization across the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR).

Concept note-3: -Stalin’s First Five-Year Plan, adopted by the party in 1928, called for rapid industrialization of the economy, with an emphasis on heavy industry. It set goals that were unrealistic-a 250 percent increase in overall industrial development and a 330 percent expansion in heavy industry alone.

Concept note-4: -Purpose was to greatly increase industrial production. more machinery, more steel production, new factories, more oil production and more electrical power plants.
