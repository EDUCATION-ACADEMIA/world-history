RUSSIAN REVOLUTION

MAJOR EVENTS IN PRE REVOLUTION RUSSIA

[SOURCES]
During the Russian Revolution of 1917, the slogan “peace, bread, and land” appealed to many Russian peasants because this slogan

(A) called for continued Russian expansion in East Asia

(B) supported an increase in the power of the Russian czar

(C) ** addressed the needs and concerns of the peasants

(D) promised to return all peasants to serfdom

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. Lenin gained the support of the Russian peasants and factory workers by promising them peace, land, and bread and preaching the ideas of Karl Marx’s communism. Lenin’s slogan gave the proletariat (poor peasants and factory workers) hope for a better future under communism.

Concept note-2: -Lenin’s promise of “Peace, Land, Bread” during the Bolshevik Revolution of 1917 was made in an effort to gain POPULAR SUPPORT to overthrow the government. The Bolshevik Party in 1917 gained the SUPPORT OF THE PEASANT CLASS because they promised them “Peace, Land, and Bread.”

Concept note-3: -Lenin used the slogan of: “Peace, land, and bread.” Lenin was referring to peace with the Germans, land that the Russian public would have, and bread would mean an end to the starvation.

Concept note-4: -Their victory was achieved with the slogans “Land and freedom!” and “Tear off the chains and the entire world will be free".

Concept note-5: -Meanwhile, Lenin and the party demanded peace, land, and bread-immediately, without further delay. The Bolshevik line won increasing support among the workers, soldiers, and peasants.
