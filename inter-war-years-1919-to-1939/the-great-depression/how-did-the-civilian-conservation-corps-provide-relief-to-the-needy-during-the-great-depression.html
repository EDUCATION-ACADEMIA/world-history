(A) By giving loans to the needy

(B) ** By creating jobs for the needy

(C) By distributing food to the needy

(D) By providing retirement funds for the needy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC), which employed young, single men at federally funded jobs on government lands. The Federal Emergency Relief Act (FERA), which gave federal grants to states that funded salaries for government workers as well as local soup kitchens and other direct-aid to the poor programs.

Concept note-2: -The Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC), established by Congress on March 31, 1933, provided jobs for young, unemployed men during the Great Depression. Over its 9-year lifespan, the CCC employed about 3 million men nationwide.

Concept note-3: -The Emergency Conservation Work Act of 1933 mandated that the Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC) recruit unemployed young men from urban areas to perform conservation work throughout the nation’s forests, parks, and fields. Concept note-4: -The CCC was part of his New Deal legislation, combating high unemployment during the Great Depression by putting hundreds of thousands of young men to work on environmental conservation projects. The CCC combined FDR’s interests in conservation and universal service for youth.

Concept note-5: -The work that the CCC did in New Mexico greatly improved the newly created state park system during the 1930s. In addition to the work done at Rattlesnake Springs and Bandelier National Monument, the men of the CCC also developed Hyde, Elephant Butte, Conchas, Bottomless Lake, and Santa Fe River Parks, among others.
