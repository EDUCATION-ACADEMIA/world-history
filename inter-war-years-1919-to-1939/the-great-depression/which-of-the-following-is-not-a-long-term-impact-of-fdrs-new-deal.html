Which of the following is NOT a long-term impact of FDR’s New Deal?

(A) Reformed the economy with a “safety net” for economic crises

(B) Recovered the economy with employment through public projects

(C) Provided relief to Americans and increased political participation from the working classes

(D) ** Removed private ownership of major industries in favor of state ownership

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the short term, New Deal programs helped improve the lives of people suffering from the events of the depression. In the long run, New Deal programs set a precedent for the federal government to play a key role in the economic and social affairs of the nation.

Concept note-2: -The New Deal did this to a remarkable degree. But FDR’s New Deal failed to cure completely the Depression-induced ills of the American economy. By 1940, the percentage of Americans without jobs remained in double digits and the American people lacked the purchasing power to jump start the economy.

Concept note-3: -The programs focused on what historians refer to as the “3 R’s": relief for the unemployed and for the poor, recovery of the economy back to normal levels, and reform of the financial system to prevent a repeat depression.
