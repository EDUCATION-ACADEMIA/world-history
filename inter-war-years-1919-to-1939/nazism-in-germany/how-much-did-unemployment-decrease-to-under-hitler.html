How much did unemployment decrease to under Hitler?

(A) 3 million

(B) ** 300, 000

(C) 6 million

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Nazis came to power in the midst of the Great Depression. The unemployment rate at that point in time was close to 30%.

Concept note-2: -Between the summer of 1929 and early 1932, German unemployment rose from just under 1.3 million to over 6 million, corresponding to a rise in the unemployment rate from 4.5 percent of the labor force to 24 percent.

Concept note-3: -The employment rate dropped to 71.8 percent in De-cember 1923, when almost one-third of all German trade union mem-bers were unemployed.

Concept note-4: -From mid-1929 to January 1933, the number of Germans who had full-time jobs fell from 20 million to 11.5 million; by the start of 1933, at least 6 million Germans were unemployed.
