When did France and Belgium take the Ruhr?

(A) 1920

(B) ** 1923

(C) 1929

(D) 1924

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On the 9 January 1923, in response to the lack of payment of reparations, France and Belgium invaded the Ruhr.

Concept note-2: -The last French troops evacuated Düsseldorf and Duisburg along with the city’s important harbour in Duisburg-Ruhrort, ending French occupation of the Ruhr region on 25 August 1925.

Concept note-3: -Ruhr occupation, (1923–25) occupation of the industrial Ruhr River valley region in Germany by French and Belgian troops. The action was provoked by German deficiencies in the coal and coke deliveries to France required by the reparations agreement after World War I.

Concept note-4: -In 1923, at the most fevered moment of the German hyperinflation, the exchange rate between the dollar and the Mark was one trillion Marks to one dollar, and a wheelbarrow full of money would not even buy a newspaper. Most Germans were taken by surprise by the financial tornado.
