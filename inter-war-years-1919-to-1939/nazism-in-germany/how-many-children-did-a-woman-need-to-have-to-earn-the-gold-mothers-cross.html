How many children did a woman need to have to earn the gold Motherâ€™s Cross?

(A) 6 or more

(B) 5 or more

(C) ** 8 or more

(D) 10 or more

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The decoration was conferred from 1939 until 1945 in three classes: bronze, silver, and gold, to Reichsdeutsche mothers who exhibited probity, exemplary motherhood, and who conceived and raised at least four or more children in the role of a parent.

Concept note-2: -The first crosses were presented in 1938 and the last in 1944, by which time approximately 4.7million had been awarded.

Concept note-3: -To encourage German women to produce more children, they were given Honour awards-bronze cross was given for four children, a silver cross for six and gold for eight or more children.

Concept note-4: -A mother could be awarded a bronze, silver, or gold cross depending on the number of children she had borne. Eight or more would entitle the woman to a gold cross, six or seven for silver, and four or five for bronze.

Concept note-5: -The Nazi government awarded honour crosses to certain mothers: bronze crosses for women who bore four children, silver crosses for women who bore six children, and gold crosses for women who bore eight or more children. Q.
