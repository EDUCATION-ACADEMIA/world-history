Why did the actions taken at the Congress of Vienna help inspire the nationalist movements across Europe in the 1800s?

(A) ** The Congress of Vienna put the absolute monarchs back in power, upsetting commoners.

(B) The Congress of Vienna imposed British rule over the entire continent of Europe, upsetting monarchs.

(C) The Congress of Vienna established democracies across Europe that had little power or organization.

(D) The Congress of Vienna prevented former leaders from regaining control of their countries, creating chaos.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The objective of the Congress was to provide a long-term peace plan for Europe by settling critical issues arising from the French Revolutionary Wars and the Napoleonic Wars through negotiation.

Concept note-2: -The Congress of Vienna redrew national boundaries with no concern for national cultures. The Congress of Vienna created a loosely organized German Confederation with Austria as the head. Germans, however, were dreaming of a strong, unified German nation. (Nationalism)

Concept note-3: -The main objective of Vienna Congress was to settle the lost peace in Europe. The French Revolutionary and the Napoleonic Wars disturbed the administration of Europe because there were many critical issues that arose. Napoleon ruled France for many years and his defeat brought an end to the 23 year long continuous war.
