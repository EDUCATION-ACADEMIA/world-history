____ was the French king during the French Revolution.

(A) Napoleon Bonaparte

(B) King Louis V

(C) ** King Louis XVI

(D) King Richard

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1754-1793. Louis XVI’s reign will forever be associated with the outbreak of the French Revolution and the end of Versailles ’ royal era. Upon coming to the throne in 1774, Louis XVI inherited a kingdom beset with serious problems.

Concept note-2: -King Louis XVI of the Bourbon dynasty inherited the French royal power towards the end of the 18th century. He lacked the experience and the prowess to handle the unusual situation of the economic and social problems his kingdom was facing at this time. Q.

Concept note-3: -King Louis XVI was the last king of France before the monarchy fell to the revolution in 1789: intellectually capable but lacking in decisiveness and authority, his regime has often been categorised as one of corruption, excess and devoid of care for his subjects.

Concept note-4: -Louis XVI, (born Aug. 23, 1754, Versailles, France-died Jan. 21, 1793, Paris), Last king of France (1774–92) in the Bourbon line preceding the French Revolution. In 1770 he married Marie-Antoinette, and in 1774 he succeeded to the throne on the death of his grandfather, Louis XV.
