EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Legislative Assembly (French: Assemblée législative) was the legislature of the Kingdom of France from 1 October 1791 to 20 September 1792 during the years of the French Revolution.

Concept note-2: -22.5. 1: The Legislative Assembly. The Legislative Assembly, the legislature of revolutionary France from October 1, 1791 to September 20, 1792, provided the focus of political debate and revolutionary law-making. However, its tenure overlapped with a period of extreme political and social chaos.

Concept note-3: -The Estates General was made up of three groups the First Estate (the clergy or church leaders), the Second Estate (the nobles), and the Third Estate (the commoners). Each group had the same amount of voting power.

Concept note-4: -Phase 1 (1789-1792) was the Moderate Phase. It included the Estates General, The Tennis Court Oath, National Assembly, Storming of The Bastille, The Great Fear, Declaration of Rights of Man and of the Citizen, Legislative Assembly and Austria War.
