FRANCE UNDER NAPOLEON

[SOURCES]
Part of what estate:Clergy

(A) ** 1st Estate

(B) 2nd Estate

(C) 3rd Estate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The First Estate was the clergy, who were people, including priests, who ran both the Catholic church and some aspects of the country. In addition to keeping registers of births, deaths and marriages, the clergy also had the power to levy a 10% tax known as the tithe.

Concept note-2: -The First Estate consisted of members of the Catholic Church (the clergy). The Second Estate consisted of members of the aristocracy (the nobility). The Third Estate comprised all other members of french society (the commoners).

Concept note-3: -France under the Ancien Régime (before the French Revolution) divided society into three estates: the First Estate (clergy); the Second Estate (nobility); and the Third Estate (commoners). The king was considered part of no estate.

Concept note-4: -The Second Estate was made up of all members of the nobility who were not members of the First Estate. This included members of the royal family, although not the King himself. The King was considered to be separate from all three estates. A depiction of French nobles from the mid-18th century.

Concept note-5: -What were the three “estates” of the Estates-General? The First Estate consisted of Roman Catholic clergy, and it was by far the smallest group represented in the Estates-General.
