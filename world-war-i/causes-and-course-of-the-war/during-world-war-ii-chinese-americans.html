During World War II, Chinese Americans

(A) were drafted in a higher proportion than any other national group

(B) received a favorable image in U.S. government propaganda

(C) saw the repeal of the Chinese Exlusion Acts

(D) ** All these answers are correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1943 the Army Air Forces organized some support units for the China-Burma-India theater, including the 14th Air Service Group, composed predominantly of Chinese-American personnel. Other Chinese-Americans trained as pilots and aircrew and fought in Europe and the Pacific.

Concept note-2: -The United States and China were allies during World War II and more than 250, 000 Americans served in what was known as the “China-Burma-India” theater. Here, a U.S. sergeant and a lieutenant, both members of the Y-Force Operations Staff, demonstrate methods of disarming the enemy with a bayonet to Chinese soldiers.
