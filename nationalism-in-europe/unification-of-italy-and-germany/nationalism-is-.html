Nationalism is ____

(A) The belief that one should be loyal to the ruler of a country

(B) ** The belief that one should be loyal to the country, not ruler

(C) The process of uniting separate areas into one country

(D) The process of gaining new colonies

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It holds that each nation should govern itself, free from outside interference (self-determination), that a nation is a natural and ideal basis for a polity, and that the nation is the only rightful source of political power.

Concept note-2: -Sectionalism is the expression of loyalty or support for a particular region of one’s country, rather than to the country as a whole. In contrast to simple feelings of local pride, sectionalism arises from deeper cultural, economic, or political differences and can lead to violent civil strife, including insurrection.

Concept note-3: -Nationalism is defined as the belief that one’s greatest loyalty should be not to a king (like Louis XVI) or emperor (like Napoleon) but to a nation of people who share a common culture and history.

Concept note-4: -also known as (not comparable) Used to introduce an alternative name; alias.
