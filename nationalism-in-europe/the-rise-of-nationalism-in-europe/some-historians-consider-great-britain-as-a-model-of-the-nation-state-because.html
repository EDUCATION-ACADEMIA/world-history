Some historians consider Great Britain as a model of the nation state because

(A) It was created after a long-drawn-out wars and political struggle

(B) It became a nation-state after forcing Scotland, Wales and Ireland to submit to it.

(C) ** It was not the result of a sudden upheaval or revolution, but the result of a long-drawn-out process.

(D) All the options

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer. Answer: (b) It become a nation-state after forcing Scotland, Wales and Ireland to submit to it.

Concept note-2: -Answer: In Britain, the formation of the nation-state was not the result of a sudden upheaval . It was a result of a long-drawn-out process . Ethnic groups inhabiting British Isles were English, Welsh, Scot or Irish .

Concept note-3: -In Britain, the formation of the nation states was the result of long drawn out process. Primary identities of the people were ethnic ones. All ethnic groupssuch as English, Welsh Scot or Irish had their own cultural and political traditions The English nation steadily grew in wealth and power.

Concept note-4: -Solution: Britain, before the 18th century, was not a nation-state. The primary identity was still in conflict as the British were still divided as a nation of the English, Scots, Welsh, and Irish. However, with the growth of the power of the English, they consequently came to dominate the other nations and islands.

Concept note-5: -Answer: True, britain formation of a nation state was a long parliamentary process. Explanation: In britain, the formation of the nation state was not the result of a sudden revolution. It was the result of a long process.
