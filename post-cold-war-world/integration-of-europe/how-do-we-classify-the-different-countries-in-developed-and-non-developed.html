How do we classify the different countries in developed and non-developed?

(A) We use the literacy rate only.

(B) We take into account the literacy rate and the human development rate.

(C) ** We use the human development rate and the GDP.

(D) We use the Gross Domestic Product only.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Countries are divided into two major categories by the United Nations, which are developed countries and developing countries. The classification of countries is based on the economic status such as GDP, GNP, per capita income, industrialization, the standard of living, etc.

Concept note-2: -A developed country-also called an industrialized country-has a mature and sophisticated economy, usually measured by gross domestic product (GDP) and/or average income per resident. Developed countries have advanced technological infrastructure and have diverse industrial and service sectors.

Concept note-3: -Countries may be classified as either developed or developing based on the gross domestic product (GDP) or gross national income (GNI) per capita, the level of industrialization, the general standard of living, and the amount of technological infrastructure, among several other potential factors.

Concept note-4: -The main difference between developed developing and underdeveloped countries is their economic status and quality of life. Developed countries have a high quality of life, developed economy, and technological infrastructure.

Concept note-5: -Accordingly, countries have been grouped as high-income, upper middle income, lower middle income and low-income (table E). To maintain compatibility with similar classifications used else-where, the threshold levels of GNI per capita are those established by the World Bank.
