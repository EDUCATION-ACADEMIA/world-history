[SOURCES]
One of the consequences of Chinese economic reforms was rise in agricultural production and income. Identify the correct order:a. High personal savingsb. Rise in agricultural production and incomec. Privatisation of agricultured. Growth in rural industry

(A) d-a-c-b

(B) ** c-b-a-d

(C) a-b-c-d

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Throughout the reform period, the government reduced tariffs and other trade barriers, with the overall tariff rate falling from 56% to 15%. By 2001, less than 40% of imports were subject to tariffs and only 9 percent of import were subject to licensing and import quotas.

Concept note-2: -China’s economic reform began with agricultural liberalisation. Between 1978 and 1983, agricultural reforms successfully transformed collectives to household-based farming and produced record-breaking growth in grain and meat output, overcoming endemic food shortages experienced since the 1950s.

Concept note-3: -The introduction of the market system, first in the rural area, and then in the urban area, must be regarded as the primary reason for the success of the economic reform.
