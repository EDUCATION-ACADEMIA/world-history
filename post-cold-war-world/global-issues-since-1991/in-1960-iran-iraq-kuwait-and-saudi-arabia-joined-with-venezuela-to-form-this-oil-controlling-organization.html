In 1960, Iran, Iraq, Kuwait and Saudi Arabia joined with Venezuela to form this oil controlling organization,

(A) Esso Research Centre

(B) Institutions of Petroleum in the Middle East

(C) ** Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC)

(D) Professional Petroleum Data Management Association

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -OPEC was established at a conference held in Baghdad September 10–14, 1960, and was formally constituted in January 1961 by five countries: Saudi Arabia, Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, and Venezuela.

Concept note-2: -The Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) is a permanent, intergovernmental Organization, created at the Baghdad Conference on September 10–14, 1960, by Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia and Venezuela.

Concept note-3: -OPEC. The Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) is an intergovernmental organization formed in September, 1960. Original members included Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia and Venezuela.

Concept note-4: -In the 1960s and 1970s, OPEC successfully restructured the global oil production system so that decision-making authority and the vast majority of profits is in the hands of oil-producing countries.
