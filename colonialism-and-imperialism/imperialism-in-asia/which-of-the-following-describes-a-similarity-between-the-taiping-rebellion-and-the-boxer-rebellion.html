Which of the following describes a similarity between the Taiping Rebellion and the Boxer Rebellion?

(A) Both hoped to remove China’s Qing dynasty from power.

(B) Both began as religious movements that eventually turned violent.

(C) Both were aimed at eliminating foreign influence on China’s government.

(D) ** Both weakened China’s government, making it difficult to resist foreign demands.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -the taiping rebellion favored missionaries, and the boxer rebellion opposed them. the taiping rebellion lasted longer than the boxer rebellion. the taiping rebellion’s result was death and starvation, but the boxer rebellion resulted in a stronger sense of nationalism in china.

Concept note-2: -The main similarity the two shared was the fact that they were both violent uprisings located in China. Adding to this, they both used fighting in order to share their beliefs and disagreements with the way things were currently operating.

Concept note-3: -Which of the following describes a major difference between the Taiping Rebellion and the Boxer Rebellion? The Taiping Rebellion targeted the Qing dynasty, while the Boxer Rebellion supported it.

Concept note-4: -’What is one similarity between the Sepoy Mutiny (1857 Indian Revolt) in India and the Boxer Rebellion in China? (AKS 44c) They resulted in the redistribution of land and military power: They were movements opposing foreign imperialists.
