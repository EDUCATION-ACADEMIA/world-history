How were efforts to Westernize problematic for the Ottoman empire?

(A) ** Adoption of Western styles of government led to resentment of foreign influence and increased tensions.

(B) They were incredibly costly and significantly increased taxes.

(C) They were unpopular with the majority of people and ineffective.

(D) They led to violent uprisings and militant extremist influences.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How were efforts to Westernize problematic for the Ottoman empire? Adoption of Western styles of government led to resentment of foreign influence and increased tensions. They were incredibly costly and significantly increased taxes. They were unpopular with the majority of people and ineffective.

Concept note-2: -Efforts to westernize were problematic for the Ottoman Empire because of issues surrounding Ottoman trade policy and difficulties in radically changing the structure and organization of the Ottoman military.

Concept note-3: -Beginning by 1840, the Ottoman Empire underwent a series of reforms known as the Tanzimat reforms. These reforms were meant to emulate strong Western countries so that the Ottoman Empire could strengthen itself like those countries had.

Concept note-4: -As many other great empires around the world, the Ottoman Empire has internal problems such as rebellions, corruption, financial weakness and military defeat which surrounded its development.

Concept note-5: -The Ottomans, in their attempts at reform and industrialization, were too late to keep pace with more developed countries regarding industrialization. Sultan Abdulhamid ended the reforms and exiled Young Turks, which were the primary advocates for reforms.
