After his own law firm in Bombay was unsuccessful, where did Gandhi move to?

(A) ** South Africa

(B) Australia

(C) London

(D) Egypt

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gandhi went to South Africa in April 1893 and stayed for a whole year in Pretoria in connection with the case of Sheth Dada Abdulla who was involved in a civil suit with his near relative Sheth Tyeb Haji Khan Mahammad. The year’s stay in Pretoria changed Gandhi’s life.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Pretoria. Gandhiji was traveling to Pretoria is a place in South Africa on June 7, 1893 night.

Concept note-3: -Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi arrived in South Africa 24 May 1893 to attend to a legal matter of Dada Abdullah Jhaveri.

Concept note-4: -After two uncertain years in India, where he was unable to start a successful law practice, he moved to South Africa in 1893 to represent an Indian merchant in a lawsuit.

Concept note-5: -Phoenix Farm is considered as the birthplace of Satyagraha. However, it was at the Tolstoy Farm, Gandhi’s second camp in South Africa, where Satyagraha was molded into a weapon of protest.
