How does the reader know there are problems with the water that Nya brings home to her family?

(A) The water is described as lukewarm, not hot or cold.

(B) The walk to get water is filled with sand and thorns.

(C) ** The people wade into the water, along with birds and cattle. The water is described as muddy.

(D) The jug she collects water in cannot be washed.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How does the reader know there are problems with the water that Nya brings home to her family? The water is described as lukewarm, not hot or cold.

Concept note-2: -Nya will lose her job when the well water is safe and plentiful. Her life in the village will change because she will no longer have to make the daily journey to the pond.

Concept note-3: -To thank others and encourage people. Nya’s story helps the reader understand the impact that Salva has on the people of southern Sudan when he returns to build wells.

Concept note-4: -How does Nya carry the water home? puts a cloth doughnut on her head, then puts jug of water on her head, balances it to walk. Why does Salva think the others left him behind?
