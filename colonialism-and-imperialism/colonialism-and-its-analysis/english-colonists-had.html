COLONIALISM AND ITS ANALYSIS

[SOURCES]
English colonists had

(A) ** more practice at government

(B) more resources

(C) more people

(D) more land

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -By the 1770s, many colonists were angry because they did not have self-government. This meant that they could not govern themselves and make their own laws. They had to pay high taxes to the king. They felt that they were paying taxes to a government where they had no representation.

Concept note-2: -As colonists grew increasingly defiant, the British government responded with punishing measures that only angered them more. In 1774, the British Parliament passed the Coercive Acts, a group of measures primarily intended to punish Boston for rebellion against the British government-namely, the Boston Tea Party.

Concept note-3: -The colonists failed to understand the difference between external and internal taxes. In principle, most Americans admitted a British right to impose duties intended to regulate colonial trade; after 1765, however, they denied Parliamentâ€™s power to tax for the purpose of raising funds or raising a revenue.

Concept note-4: -The English colonists, on the other hand, enjoyed far more freedom and were able to govern themselves as long as they followed English law and were loyal to the king. In addition, unlike France and Spain, England encouraged immigration from other nations, thus boosting its colonial popula-tion.
