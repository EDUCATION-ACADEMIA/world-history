COLONIALISM AND ITS ANALYSIS

[SOURCES]
Local Rulers are allowed to keep their authority and status in a new colonial setting.

(A) Imperialism

(B) Direct Rule

(C) Protectorate

(D) ** Indirect Rule

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Under indirect rule, local rulers were allowed to keep their authority and status in a new colonial setting. This made access to the regionâ€™s natural resources easier. Indirect rule was cheaper because fewer officials had to be trained. It also affected local culture less.

Concept note-2: -Indirect rule allowed local rulers to maintain their possession of authority and states in a new colonial setting. Direct rule replaced local elites with a new set of officials brought from the mother country, mostly because they local elites resisted the foreign conquest.

Concept note-3: -Indirect rule was the plan to use existing tribal structures and traditions as conduits for establishing rules and regulations while English officials worked behind the scenes and could exercise a veto power.

Concept note-4: -Indirect and direct rule thus had opposite effects on pre-colonial structures of power: indirect rule aimed to preserve them, while direct rule was intended to eradicate and replace them with a new colonial order.

Concept note-5: -Indirect rule was a system of governance used by the British and others to control parts of their colonial empires, particularly in Africa and Asia, which was done through pre-existing indigenous power structures.
