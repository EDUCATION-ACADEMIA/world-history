Which groups mixed to shape the culture of Latin America today?

(A) ** African, European, Native

(B) African, European

(C) European, Native

(D) African, Native

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mestizos are people of mixed indigenous and European ancestry. Today, mestizos make up large parts of the populations of many South American countries, such as Paraguay (95 percent), Ecuador (65 percent), and Colombia (58 percent). Mestizos were at the heart of South America’s revolutionary movement.

Concept note-2: -Latin America also has diversity in its races. Europeans, Africans, and Native Americans were the largest groups. Members of these groups have intermarried and developed unique cultures over time.

Concept note-3: -The richness of Latin American culture is the product of many influences, including: Spanish and Portuguese culture, owing to the region’s history of colonization, settlement and continued immigration from Spain and Portugal.

Concept note-4: -The ethno-racial composition of modern-day Latin American nations combines diverse Indigenous American populations, with influence from Iberian and other Western European colonizers, and equally diverse African groups brought to the Americas as slave labor, and also recent immigrant groups from all over the world.

Concept note-5: -To sum up, the heritage of Latin America blends indigenous, European, African, and Asian peoples, languages, and cultural traditions. There is no one Latin America, or Latino or Latin American culture-rather, it is all these things.
