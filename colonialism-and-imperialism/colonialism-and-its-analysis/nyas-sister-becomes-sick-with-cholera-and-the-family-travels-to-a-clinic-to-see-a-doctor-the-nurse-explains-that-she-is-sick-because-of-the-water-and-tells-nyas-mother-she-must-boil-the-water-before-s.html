Nya’s sister becomes sick with cholera and the family travels to a clinic to see a doctor. The nurse explains that she is sick because of the water and tells Nya’s mother she must boil the water before she uses it. What is the problem with boiling water for Nya’s family?

(A) They have no pots to boil water in.

(B) There is no way for them to make a fire.

(C) ** Because they can collect only a little water, they will not have enough left after boiling.

(D) If they start a fire, it will burn their house down.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nurse explains that she is sick because of the water and tells Nya’s mother she must boil the water before she uses it. What is the problem with boiling water for Nya’s family? They have no pots to boil water in. There is no way for them to make a fire.

Concept note-2: -Akeer is Nya’s sister. She is sick because she drank contaminated water. Her family took her to the medical clinic, which was several days’ journey away.

Concept note-3: -Nya and Salva would not have been friends because they are from different rival tribes. (1985) 3.

Concept note-4: -What choice does Nya’s family make about Akeer’s illness? To take her to a medical clinic. What must the family do so Akeer does not get sick again? Boil the water so it is clean and she will not get sick again.

Concept note-5: -Summary: Chapter 4, Nya: Southern Sudan, 2008 Nya sits in the shade and eats a bowl of boiled sorghum and milk. Inside, her mother nurses her brother. She tells Nya to take her younger sister, Akeer, back to the pond. Nya thinks five-year-old Akeer is too young, but their mother insists that Akeer needs to learn.
