The Royatwari system was implemented mainly in

(A) ** Bombay Deccan

(B) Calcutta and Northern India

(C) Northern India only

(D) Bihar and Assam.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Complete answer: The Ryotwari System was introduced in 1820 by Sir Thomas Munro, the then governor of Madras the system was practised in the Madras and Bombay region of the country.

Concept note-2: -Ryotwari system This system of land revenue was instituted in the late 18th century by Sir Thomas Munro, Governor of Madras in 1820. This was practised in the Madras and Bombay areas, as well as Assam and Coorg provinces. In this system, the peasants or cultivators were regarded as the owners of the land.

Concept note-3: -Ryotwari System was introduced by Thomas Munro in 1820. Major areas of introduction include Madras, Bombay, parts of Assam and Coorgh provinces of British India. In Ryotwari System the ownership rights were handed over to the peasants. British Government collected taxes directly from the peasants.

Concept note-4: -The Ryotwari system is associated with the name of Thomas Munro, who was appointed Governor of Madras in May 1820. Subsequently, the Ryotwari system was extended to the Bombay area. Munro gradually reduced the rate of taxation from one half to one third of the gross produce, even then an excessive tax.

Concept note-5: -The correct answer is the Ryotwari Settlement. It is Introduced in Bombay and Madras.
