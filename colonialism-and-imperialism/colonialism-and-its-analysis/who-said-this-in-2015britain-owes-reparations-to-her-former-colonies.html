Who said this in 2015:“Britain owes reparations to her former colonies”

(A) Manmohan Singh

(B) Narendra Modi

(C) Rahul Gandhi

(D) ** Shashi Tharoor

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During a debate at the Oxford Union on 28 May 2015, the Indian Member of Parliament, diplomat and writer Shashi Tharoor delivered a speech supporting the motion “Britain owes reparations to her former colonies".

Concept note-2: -What does reparations mean? Reparations are forms of compensation provided to those who have suffered wrongdoing or to their descendants. The term is especially used to refer to payments made (or proposed to be made) in the aftermath of war, slavery, or other forms of wide-scale systemic injustice.

Concept note-3: -For example we can say Shashi Tharoor is a sesquipedalian person. The word means the pretentious or showy display of wealth and luxury particularly designed to impress.

Concept note-4: -The British destabilised crop patterns by forced commercial cropping, and left Indians more prone to famines. Between 12 and 29 million Indians died of starvation while India was under the control of the British Empire.

Concept note-5: -Shashi Tharoor (IPA: [ʃɐʃi t̪ɐɾuːr]; SHUH-shee thǝ-ROOR; born 9 March 1956 in London, England ) is an Indian former international civil servant, diplomat, bureaucrat and politician, writer and public intellectual who has been serving as Member of Parliament for Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, since 2009.
