(A) Patriots received better treatment than Loyalists received.

(C) Everyone living in the thirteen colonies favored independence from Great Britain.

(D) ** The issue of independence from Great Britain caused sharp differences among the American colonists.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Samuel Adams was one of Boston’s most prominent revolutionary leaders. He was known for his ability to harness popular resentment against Parliament’s authority to tax the colonies in a productive manner. His role in the origins of the American War of Independence cannot be understated.

Concept note-2: -Samuel Adams was born on September 22, 1722, in Quincy, Massachusetts. He entered Harvard College at the age of 14. During the 1760s, Adams became a leader of the Patriot resistance to the British government’s attempt to tax the American colonies.

Concept note-3: -Despite very little documentary evidence as to the origins of the organization, Boston Patriot Samuel Adams is often credited as being the founder and leader of the Sons of Liberty. The Sons of Liberty was most likely organized in the summer of 1765 as a means to protest the passing of the Stamp Act of 1765.
