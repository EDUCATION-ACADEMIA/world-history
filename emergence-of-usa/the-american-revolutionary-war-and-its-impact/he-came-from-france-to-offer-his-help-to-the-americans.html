He came from France to offer his help to the Americans:

(A) Lord Dunmore

(B) Thomas Paine

(C) Friedrich von Steuben

(D) ** Marquis de Lafayette

(E) Charles Cornwallis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1790, Gilbert du Motier-better known as the Marquis de Lafayette-sent the key to George Washington on behalf of the people of France. To understand why Lafayette did this, it is necessary to understand the special relationship that developed between Washington and Lafayette during the American Revolution.

Concept note-2: -During Lafayette’s time in France, he played an extremely crucial role in securing 6, 000 French troops for the American cause. Returning to the colonies in 1780, Lafayette’s news of French aid greatly improved American moral. Since his departure, the crucial field of battle had moved to the south.

Concept note-3: -Lafayette would resume his position as a major general of American forces, serving as liaison between Rochambeau and Washington, who would be in command of both nations’ forces. In March 1780, he departed from Rochefort for America aboard the frigate Hermione, arriving in Boston on 27 April 1780.

Concept note-4: -Lafayette came to Williamsburg during preparations for the Virginia Campaign of 1781. At Yorktown, Lafayette helped to corner Britain’s Lord Cornwallis, whose surrender after several days of siege was a fatal blow that ensured the American victory.

Concept note-5: -George Washington met the nineteen-year-old Marquis de Lafayette on August 5, 1777, less than a week after the Continental Congress appointed the young Frenchman to be a volunteer Major General in the Continental Army. Lafayette was assigned to serve on Washington’s staff.
