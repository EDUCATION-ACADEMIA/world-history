A MAIN idea of the Declaration of Independence is that

(A) people can rebel against their government any time they want.

(B) ** government gets its authority or power from the consent of the people who are being governed.

(C) taxes should not be increased.

(D) government should not interfere in economic activities.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Declaration of Independence included these three major ideas: People have certain Inalienable Rights including Life, Liberty and Pursuit of Happiness. All Men are created equal. Individuals have a civic duty to defend these rights for themselves and others.

Concept note-2: -On June 7, 1776, Richard Henry Lee introduced a resolution “that these united colonies are and of right ought to be free and independent states.” They appointed a Committee of Five to write an announcement explaining the reasons for independence.

Concept note-3: -“Consent of the governed” refers to the idea that a government’s legitimacy and moral right to use state power is justified and lawful only when consented, or agreed to, by the people over which that political power is exercised.
