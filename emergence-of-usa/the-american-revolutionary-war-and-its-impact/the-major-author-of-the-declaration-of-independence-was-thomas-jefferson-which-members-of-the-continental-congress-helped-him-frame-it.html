(A) John Hancock & Samuel Adams

(B) ** John Adams & Benjamin Franklin

(C) Benjamin Harrison & Richard Henry Lee

(D) Thomas Stone & Philip Livingston

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Committee of Five . The committee consisted of two New England men, John Adams of Massachusetts and Roger Sherman of Connecticut; two men from the Middle Colonies, Benjamin Franklin of Pennsylvania and Robert R. Livingston of New York; and one southerner, Thomas Jefferson of Virginia.

Concept note-2: -Adams was a Massachusetts delegate to both the First and Second Continental Congresses. He was a leading proponent of independence from Great Britain, and served on the five-man committee (which included Thomas Jefferson) assigned to draft the Declaration of Independence.

Concept note-3: -Despite this disagreement, Congress did nominate a drafting committee-the Committee of Five (John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Thomas Jefferson, Robert Livingston, and Roger Sherman)-to compose a declaration of independence.

Concept note-4: -On July 4, 1776, the Second Continental Congress unanimously adopted the Declaration of Independence, announcing the coloniesâ€™ separation from Great Britain.

Concept note-5: -(1743-1826) Virginian Thomas Jefferson was one of the youngest members of the Continental Congress, but upon his arrival in 1775 he already had a reputation as a fine writer.
