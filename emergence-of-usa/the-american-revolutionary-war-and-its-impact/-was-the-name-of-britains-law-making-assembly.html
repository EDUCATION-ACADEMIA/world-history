____ was the name of Britain’s law making assembly.

(A) House of Burgess

(B) Meeting House

(C) ** Parliament

(D) Congress

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Parliament of England was the legislature of the Kingdom of England from the 13th century until 1707 when it was replaced by the Parliament of Great Britain. Parliament evolved from the great council of bishops and peers that advised the English monarch.

Concept note-2: -The business of Parliament takes place in two Houses: the House of Commons and the House of Lords. Their work is similar: making laws (legislation), checking the work of the government (scrutiny), and debating current issues.

Concept note-3: -The British Parliament, often referred to as the “Mother of Parliaments, ” consists of the sovereign, the House of Lords, and the House of Commons.

Concept note-4: -An Act of Parliament creates a new law or changes an existing law. An Act is a Bill that has been approved by both the House of Commons and the House of Lords and been given Royal Assent by the Monarch. Taken together, Acts of Parliament make up what is known as Statute Law in the UK.

Concept note-5: -Originally called the House of Parliament, it was designed by the British architects Edwin Lutyens and Herbert Baker in 1912-1913 as part of their wider mandate to construct a new administrative capital city for India.
