[SOURCES]
The name the colonists gave to the laws that Britain imposed on the colonies as punishment after the Boston Tea Party. 1. British troops were sent to Boston. 2. Massachusetts was put under British control3. The port of Boston was closed until colonists paid for the tea they destroyed.

(A) Tea Act

(B) Stamp Act

(C) ** Intolerable Acts

(D) Declaratory Acts

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Coercive Acts of 1774, known as the Intolerable Acts in the American colonies, were a series of four laws passed by the British Parliament to punish the colony of Massachusetts Bay for the Boston Tea Party.

Concept note-2: -The Coercive Acts, which were called the Intolerable Acts by the American colonists, were passed by Parliament in 1774 in response to colonial resistance to British rule.

Concept note-3: -The Intolerable Acts were a series of punitive laws passed by the British Parliament in 1774 after the Boston Tea Party. The laws aimed to punish Massachusetts colonists for their defiance in the Tea Party protest of the Tea Act, a tax measure enacted by Parliament in May 1773.

Concept note-4: -The Stamp Act, Sugar Act, Townshend Acts, and Intolerable Acts are four acts that contributed to the tension and unrest among colonists that ultimately led to the American Revolution. The first act was the Sugar Act, which was passed in 1764. This placed a tax on sugar and molasses imported into the colonies.
