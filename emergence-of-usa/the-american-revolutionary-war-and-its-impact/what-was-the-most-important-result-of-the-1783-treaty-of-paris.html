What was the most important result of the 1783 Treaty of Paris?

(A) Britain gained American territory.

(B) ** Britain recognized American independence.

(C) France agreed to fight with the Americans

(D) France decided to trade with the Americans.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Two crucial provisions of the treaty were British recognition of U.S. independence and the delineation of boundaries that would allow for American western expansion. The treaty is named for the city in which it was negotiated and signed.

Concept note-2: -The Treaty of Paris was signed by U.S. and British Representatives on September 3, 1783, ending the War of the American Revolution. Based on a1782 preliminary treaty, the agreement recognized U.S. independence and granted the U.S. significant western territory.

Concept note-3: -The Treaty of Paris of 1763 ended the French and Indian War/Seven Yearsâ€™ War between Great Britain and France, as well as their respective allies. In the terms of the treaty, France gave up all its territories in mainland North America, effectively ending any foreign military threat to the British colonies there.

Concept note-4: -Secured fishing rights to the Grand Banks and other waters off the British-Canadian coastline for American boats. Opened up the Mississippi River to navigation by citizens of both the United States and Great Britain. Resolved issues with American debts owed to British creditors.

Concept note-5: -Native Americans were not included in the Treaty of Paris (1783), which concluded the American Revolution. The end of fighting presented them with a difficult path as they struggled to protect their homelands from their growing insignificance within the shifting international politics of eighteenth-century America.
