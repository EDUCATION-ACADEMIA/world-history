HOW DID THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION INFLUENCE THE FRENCH REVOLUTION

[SOURCES]
Locke believed the roles within the government should be separated into two parts (makes laws and enforces laws).

(A) ** Legislative and Executive

(B) Judicial and Executive

(C) Checks and Balances

(D) Legislative, Executive, and Judicial

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The purpose of government, Locke wrote, is to secure and protect the God-given inalienable natural rights of the people. For their part, the people must obey the laws of their rulers. Thus, a sort of contract exists between the rulers and the ruled.

Concept note-2: -Locke famously wrote that man has three natural rights: life, liberty and property. In his “Thoughts Concerning Education” (1693), Locke argued for a broadened syllabus and better treatment of students-ideas that were an enormous influence on Jean-Jacques Rousseau’s novel “Emile” (1762).

Concept note-3: -Locke also defends the principle of majority rule and the separation of legislative and executive powers.

Concept note-4: -Theorists such as the English philosopher John Locke believed that if a ruler goes against natural law and fails to protect “life, liberty, and property, ” then the people are justified in overthrowing the existing state.
