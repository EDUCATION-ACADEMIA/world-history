HOW DID THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION INFLUENCE THE FRENCH REVOLUTION

[SOURCES]
According to Locke if the government fails to protect peoples rights the people can ____

(A) People can not question the government

(B) People can do what they want

(C) ** People can change the government

(D) People have no say

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -But, Locke concluded, if a government persecutes its people with “a long train of abuses” over an extended period, the people have the right to resist that government, alter or abolish it, and create a new political system. Jefferson adopted John Locke’s theory of natural rights to provide a reason for revolution.

Concept note-2: -Locke believed that in a state of nature, people protect their natural rights – life, liberty and property-by using their own strength and skill. The weaker and less skilled would find it difficult to protect their rights.

Concept note-3: -For Locke, the government must be a “neutral judge” of law with no right to interfere in the lives of the individual. The most radical idea to come from Locke’s pen was the idea of governmental legitimacy. Locke believed that a government should be beholden to the people rather than vice-versa.

Concept note-4: -Government, he argued, should be limited to securing the life and property of its citizens, and is only necessary because in an ideal, anarchic state of nature, various problems arise that would make life more insecure than under the protection of a minimal state.

Concept note-5: -In politics, Locke is best known as a proponent of limited government. He uses a theory of natural rights to argue that governments have obligations to their citizens, have only limited powers over their citizens, and can ultimately be overthrown by citizens under certain circumstances.
