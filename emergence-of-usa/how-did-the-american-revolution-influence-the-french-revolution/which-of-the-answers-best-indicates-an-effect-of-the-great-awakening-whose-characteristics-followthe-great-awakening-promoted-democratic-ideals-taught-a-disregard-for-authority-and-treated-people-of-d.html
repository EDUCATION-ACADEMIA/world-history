HOW DID THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION INFLUENCE THE FRENCH REVOLUTION

[SOURCES]
Which of the answers best indicates an effect of the Great Awakening whose characteristics follow:The Great Awakening-promoted democratic ideals, taught a disregard for authority, and treated people of differing genders and races as equals.

(A) slave trade was abolished

(B) women gained the right to vote

(C) ** colonists began calling for revolution

(D) colonies established state-supported churches

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Effects of the Great Awakening The Great Awakening notably altered the religious climate in the American colonies. Ordinary people were encouraged to make a personal connection with God, instead of relying on a minister. Newer denominations, such as Methodists and Baptists, grew quickly.

Concept note-2: -We have already mentioned the most important causes for the beginning of the Great Awakening; there were significantly fewer church attendances throughout the country, many people were also bored and unsatisfied with the way the sermons were conducted, and they criticized the lack of enthusiasm from their preachers.

Concept note-3: -The Great Awakening was an outburst of Protestant Revivalism in the eighteenth century. The beliefs of the New Lights of the First Great Awakening competed with the more conservative religion of the first colonists, who were known as Old Lights.

Concept note-4: -Although the Great Awakening was a reaction against the Enlightenment, it was also a long term cause of the Revolution. Before, ministers represented an upper class of sorts. Awakening ministers were not always ordained, breaking down respect for betters.
