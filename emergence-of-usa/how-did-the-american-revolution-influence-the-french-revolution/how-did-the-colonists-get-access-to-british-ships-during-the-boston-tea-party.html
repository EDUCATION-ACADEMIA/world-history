HOW DID THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION INFLUENCE THE FRENCH REVOLUTION

[SOURCES]
How did the colonists get access to British ships during the Boston Tea Party?

(A) They attacked and killed the British guards.

(B) They paid the ship’s captain to let them on.

(C) ** They dressed like Mohawk Indians.

(D) They pretended to be British soldiers.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In Boston Harbor, a group of Massachusetts colonists disguised as Mohawk Indians board three British tea ships and dump 342 chests of tea into the harbor.

Concept note-2: -Q. How did the colonists get access to British ships during the Boston Tea Party? They attacked and killed British guards. They paid the ship’s captain to let them on.

Concept note-3: -In an effort to hide their true identities, many of the Sons of Liberty attempted to pass themselves off as Mohawk Indians because if caught for their actions they would have faced severe punishment. Reports from the time describe the participants as dressed as Mohawks or Narragansett Indians.

Concept note-4: -To protest the Tea Act of 1773, colonists disguised as Mohawks raided ships and dumped ninety thousand pounds of tea into Boston Harbor.

Concept note-5: -The Boston Tea Party was a political protest that occurred on December 16, 1773, at Griffin’s Wharf in Boston, Massachusetts. American colonists, frustrated and angry at Britain for imposing “taxation without representation, ” dumped 342 chests of tea, imported by the British East India Company into the harbor.
